History

Whitewater rafting began as a practical way to navigate rivers for exploration and transport. Early adventurers used simple wooden rafts to travel through fast-moving waters, especially in remote regions where rivers were the main routes. Over time, these journeys evolved from necessity into exciting challenges.

During the 20th century, rafting became a popular recreational sport. With the development of safer inflatable rafts and guided tours, people from around the world began to enjoy the thrill of river rapids. Today, rafting is both an adventure activity and a way to connect with nature.
